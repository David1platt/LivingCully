Mosaic Arts

Mary Topagna graduated from the Art Academy of Cinncinnati with a BFA in Photography. She moved to Portland in 1991, and worked as a freelance photographer for The Oregonian. Over time Topagna slowly transitioned into exhibiting mosaic art pieces, and taking on a number of large and small commissioned projects.

Mary operated a storefront/gallery for 12 years off of 29th and Killingsworth until operating costs became to expensive. Now she maintains a private gallery/workspace. Her mosaic works cover a broad range of secular and religious portraiture. Her work also includes a number of ornamental objects ranging from crosses and rosaries to tables and lamps. These art objects are created using a number of interesting found and recycled materials to create detailed designs and imagery based on striking photography.
